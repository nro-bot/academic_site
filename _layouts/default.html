Rui Ouyang

I am a PhD student at the Harvard School of Engineering and Applied Sciences, where I work on tactile perception in robotics. My PhD advisor is Prof. Edward Adelson at MIT.

I have previously worked with Prof. Robert Howe at Harvard University and Prof. Robert Platt at Northeastern University. Prior to graduate school, I worked with Dr. Albert Wang in the MIT Biomimetics Lab (headed by Prof. Sangbae Kim). I also worked at the bioinformatics startup Curoverse and the Personal Genome Project, both under Dr. Alexander Zaranek.

In a prior life, my friend Cappie Pomeroy and I founded a startup , NarwhalEdu, focused on combining online education and hands-on hardware kits. I escaped with a BS in Mechanical Engineering (concentration in robotics) from MIT.

This website focuses on my academic work; for other work, please see my engineering portfolio. My academic pseudonym (to keep citations consistent) is Rui; in daily life, I also go by Nancy and Nao.
